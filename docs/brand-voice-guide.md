# SaborConFlow Dance - Brand Voice Guide

## Core Voice Characteristics

### Tone
- **Encouraging**: Celebrate effort and progress
- **Knowledgeable**: Demonstrate expertise without being pedantic
- **Approachable**: Professional yet warm and friendly
- **Excited**: Genuine enthusiasm for dance and teaching

### Personality
- Systematic and clear (tech background influence)
- Culturally respectful without claiming authenticity
- Community-focused
- Progress-oriented

### Length Guidelines
- Keep concise for cross-platform compatibility
- Facebook and WhatsApp should use same content
- Aim for 150-250 words for standard announcements
- Can be shorter for special announcements

### Paragraph Flow
- Opening: Greeting + gratitude
- Context: Optional reference to previous class
- Main content: What will be covered
- Focus section: Bullet points
- Closing: Excitement statement + signature

## Time References

Use natural, conversational timing:
- "This Sunday" (for same-week announcements)
- "Tomorrow" (for day-before announcements)
- "This week" (general)
- Specific dates when needed

## What to AVOID

1. **Authenticity claims**: Never claim to teach "authentic" Cuban dance in a way that appropriates culture
2. **Excessive formality**: Keep it warm, not corporate
3. **Long-winded explanations**: Be concise
4. **Overused phrases**: Vary your language
5. **Technical jargon without context**: Define terms when introducing new ones
6. **Excessive emojis**: Keep them minimal
7. **Generic motivational quotes**: Be specific to dance and your community

## What to EMBRACE

1. **Specific move names**: Use proper Cuban terminology
2. **Musical references**: Connect to specific songs when relevant
3. **Community celebration**: Acknowledge group achievements
4. **Personal excitement**: Show genuine enthusiasm
5. **Clear expectations**: Tell students what they'll learn
6. **Encouragement**: Make students feel capable

## Choreo Team Exception

Choreo Team messages have a different format:
- No greeting
- No gratitude statement
- No signature
- More direct, team-focused tone
- Can be longer for logistics
- Address as "Team" not "everyone"
